How

I made a Python solution that reads data from a pre defined data model. This can still change The scripts in the back-end read the data and are able to plot it on a pre-defined map.

Step 1 - Create a map

There is now the option to find your address on a map - only for Belgium.

Click on map for a pop-up with geo-data. The links do not work for now Sorry, for now write down. And go to the form

Go to the Maps - Forms and create your map location. Use the Geo data and paste it in the correct fields. The first is latitude and second is longitude. The format is a floating number (point decimal). Provide a name for your map. Something that you will remember.

Step 2 - Create jars

Then you go to Google again and locate the location of your jars. Again, right click and copy the geo data. I prefer to use the csv text upload in the "CSV Upload". This is rapid and easy. Read on that page how to upload the csv data. Remember, each location must be unique and each jar has a unique name. Add your estimated distance and flight vector. It is now possible to add a latitude or longitude as komma or as a 'dot' seperated value. In the backend the code will change this for your. Simply click on a map in Google or on the one provided in this tool. Copy the shown data in the popup to your desktop.

A vector is found with a compass. This is a number (type integer) that points in degrees. North is "zero/360", south is "180"

Step 3 - Put Jar on Map

Go to jars table. On the far right you click on the "add on map" button. Check if the correct jar is chosen and choose the map. Just click "add".

Step 4 - Generate Map

Go to the Map Generator and select your map. Click on the button. Other solution is to go to the map table and click on the button to generate your map. This is probably a quicker solution as you can already see what jars are on your map.

What

Welcome to this quick and dirty solution for mapping your 'jars' data on a map. This project is a Python project based upon Flask, Flask-SQLAlchemy, Marshmallow, Pandas and many more. I suggest to have a look on the Git source code to understand the build. For Flask I used the blueprints. Blueprinting allows to grow your app and even re-use 'blocks' of code in other projects quickly. There is also a CRUD API active in the background. I did not made any 'documentation" about this.

The good thing about this project?

It keeps your data... Just update your 'jars' with new observations. Change the heading, change the estimated distance. Generate your map again with your new information. Good?

Why

Welcome to this quick and dirty solution for mapping your 'jars' data on a map. I made this project because of the variety of tools and solutions that exist to plot your tracking information. There is never a good solution, or you have to pay for it. This one is currently free. Though I will not stop you to contact me and pay me :-)
